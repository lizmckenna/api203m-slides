Theory

Two General Approaches to Mixed Methods

  • →Mixed data collection — Combine two or more kinds of data (e.g., geoinformatics/remote sensing + fieldwork and interviews)
  • →Mixed data analysis — Two or more analytical strategies (e.g., quantitative text analysis/NLP + archival/content analysis)
  • →Example: Siegel et al. (2022) — geoinformatics + fieldwork
  • →Example: Munir (2019) — sentiment analysis + content analysis
04
Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead
Theory

When Are Mixed Methods Valuable?

  • →Confirmation — Verify findings from one type of data with another type to measure phenomena (triangulation). Conflicting findings from disparate data sources can be generative as well, e.g., Pager (2005) audit study of employment discrimination.
  • →Complementarity — One type of data can "compensate" for the weaknesses of the other. Examples: Rao, Ananthpur, and Malik (2017); Cohen (2016); Han, McKenna, Oyakaya (2021).

Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead
Theory

Design and Sequencing

  • →Convergent design — QUANT + QUAL conducted simultaneously, then comparison of results (e.g., Pager 2003)
  • →Sequential explanatory design — QUANT → QUAL: Interpretation of quantitative results using qualitative research (e.g., Taylor et al. 2009)
  • →Sequential exploratory design — QUAL → QUANT: Transfer or generalization of qualitative results using quantitative results (e.g., Small et al. 2008)

Theory

Why Identify What Constitutes Sound Qualitative Work?

  • →As a policymaker or leader — You must decide whether to allocate resources to an analysis, evaluate its design, and assess its likelihood of producing defensible evidence
  • →As a researcher or practitioner — You want to produce work that stands up to scrutiny to make a persuasive case to policymakers, practitioners, and others about a policy, finding, or recommendation

Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead
Theory

Prior Criteria: King, Keohane, and Verba (1994)

Reliability

The process of data collection and analysis should yield the same results if repeated by the same researcher or by others

Lack of Bias

Qualitative research should strive for objectivity and impartiality, minimizing the influence of the researcher's personal beliefs, values, or predispositions

Efficiency

Research should answer the research question as directly as possible, without unnecessary expenditure of time, effort, or financial resources

Why might these criteria have been considered controversial?
10
Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead
Theory

KKV Criteria vs. Desmond's Evicted

  • →Reliability? — Desmond's deeply embedded ethnography of eviction experiences in Milwaukee can't be precisely replicated
  • →Efficiency? — Extensive fieldwork is inefficient compared to quantitative survey methods
  • →Lack of bias? — Desmond's direct involvement with participants and empathetic portrayal may introduce subjectivity. Yet the work provided invaluable policy insights: highlighting previously unnoticed dynamics of housing instability, shifting policy discourse toward recognizing eviction as a cause of poverty, and leading to tangible impacts such as advocacy for tenants' rights and legal representation policies.

Indicators of Sound Qualitative Research

Small and Calarco's framework for evaluating ethnographic and interview research:

  • →Cognitive empathy — The degree to which the researcher understood those observed close to how they understand themselves
  • →Heterogeneity — The extent to which the people or places depicted are represented as diverse
  • →Palpability — The degree to which the evidence presented is not abstract but concrete
  • →Follow-up — The extent to which the researcher collected data to answer unanticipated questions that arose during data collection
  • →Self-awareness — The degree to which the researcher recognized how their own identity affects access, interpretation, and data
13
Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead
Theory

Exposure: The Precondition

  • →Exposure is the precondition for all good qualitative research and analysis — the researcher must spend sufficient time in the field or with participants
  • →Without adequate exposure, none of the five indicators can be meaningfully achieved

Theory

Heterogeneity

  • →The extent to which the people or places depicted are represented as diverse
  • →"Among both interview-based and participant observation studies, those that uncover little or no ______ are ultimately unconvincing." — Small and Calarco (2022)

Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead
Theory

Palpability

  • →The degree to which the evidence presented is not abstract but concrete
  • →Highly palpable evidence brings the reader close to what the participants actually experience — Small and Calarco (2022)
16
Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead
Theory

Cognitive Empathy

  • →The degree to which the researcher came to understand those observed close to how they understand themselves
  • →What people perceive, what it means to them, what motivates them — see what they see, understand what they understand
17
Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead
Theory

Follow-up

  • →The extent to which the researcher collected data to answer unanticipated questions that arose during data collection — Small and Calarco (2022)
  • →Good qualitative researchers remain responsive to surprises in the field and adjust their inquiry accordingly
18
Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead
Theory

Self-Awareness

  • →"Who [a researcher is] — their gender, race, height and weight, level of extraversion, grace or awkwardness, hairstyle, clothing, vocabulary and intonation, class background, religion, fundamental beliefs, sympathy or disdain for others, and ignorance, and every other aspect of their identity — affects how easily they reach others, what others do and say in front of them, and how they interpret what they have heard or observed." (p. 119)
  • →Sound qualitative research requires the researcher to acknowledge and reflect on these dynamics
