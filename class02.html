When Are Mixed Methods Valuable?

Confirmation (Triangulation)

•Verify findings from one type of data with another type
•Conflicting findings can be generative
•Pager (2005): audit study of employment discrimination — field experiment + in-depth interviews revealed how race and criminal record interact in hiring
07
Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead

Pager Study: Why Both Methods Mattered

What the audit experiment showed
What the interviews added
•Discrimination was real in actual hiring
•How employers made sense of applicants; helped explain heterogeneity in practices among employers
•A criminal record sharply reduced callbacks
•Surfaced employers' stereotypes and ideas about risk, trust, and reliability
•Black applicants with a record faced the greatest penalty
•Revealed the gap between stated attitudes and actual behavior
•The experiment identified and quantified the pattern
•The interviews helped uncover the mechanisms, meanings, and justifications behind that pattern

When Are Mixed Methods Valuable?

Complementarity

•One type of data can compensate for weaknesses of another
•Cohen (2016) on wartime sexual violence
•Han, McKenna, Oyakawa (2021) on social movement power-building
•Rao, Ananthpur, and Malik (2017): RCT of civic engagement in South India — quant measured participation rates, qual revealed how caste and gender norms shaped who actually participated and why

Why Identify What Constitutes Sound Qualitative Work?

As a policymaker or leader

You must decide whether to allocate resources to an analysis, evaluate its design, and assess the defensibility of the evidence.

As a researcher or practitioner

You want to produce work that stands up to scrutiny and makes a persuasive case to policymakers, practitioners, and others.

Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead
Theory

Prior Criteria: King, Keohane, and Verba (1994)

Reliability

The process of data collection and analysis should yield the same results if repeated by the same researcher or by others

Lack of Bias

Qualitative research should strive for objectivity and impartiality, minimizing the influence of the researcher's personal beliefs, values, or predispositions

Efficiency

Research should answer the research question as directly as possible, without unnecessary expenditure of time, effort, or financial resources

What do you think of these criteria as applied to qual research?
14
Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead
Theory

KKV Criteria Applied: Desmond's Evicted

✗Reliability: Desmond's deeply embedded ethnography of eviction experiences in Milwaukee can't be precisely replicated
✗Efficiency: Extensive fieldwork is inefficient compared to quantitative survey methods
✗Lack of bias: Desmond's direct involvement with participants and landlords, and empathetic portrayal of their experiences, may introduce subjectivity, violating positivist standards.

Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead
Theory

Yet, Invaluable Policy Insights

✓Highlighted previously unnoticed dynamics of housing instability, directly influencing housing policies.
✓Shifted policy discourse toward recognizing eviction as a cause—not just a consequence—of poverty.
✓Led to tangible impacts, such as advocacy for tenants' rights, rental assistance programs, and legal representation policies nationwide.

Theory

Indicators of Sound Qualitative Research

Small and Calarco's framework for evaluating ethnographic and interview research

1. Cognitive Empathy

Understood those observed close to how they understand themselves

2. Heterogeneity

People or places depicted are represented as diverse

3. Palpability

Evidence presented is not abstract but concrete

4. Follow-up

Collected data to answer unanticipated questions during research

5. Self-awareness

Recognized how the researcher's own identity affects access, interpretation, and data

Theory

Exposure: The Precondition

Exposure is the precondition for all good qualitative research and analysis — the researcher must spend sufficient time in the field or with participants.
Without adequate exposure, none of the five indicators can be meaningfully achieved.

Theory

Heterogeneity

  • →The extent to which the people or places depicted are represented as diverse
  • →"Among both interview-based and participant observation studies, those that uncover little or no ______ are ultimately unconvincing." — Small and Calarco (2022)

Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead
Theory

Palpability

  • →The degree to which the evidence presented is not abstract but concrete
  • →Highly palpable evidence brings the reader close to what the participants actually experience — Small and Calarco (2022)
25
Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead
Theory

Cognitive Empathy

  • →The degree to which the researcher came to understand those observed close to how they understand themselves
  • →What people perceive, what it means to them, what motivates them — see what they see, understand what they understand
26
Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead
Theory

Follow-up

  • →The extent to which the researcher collected data to answer unanticipated questions that arose during data collection — Small and Calarco (2022)
  • →Good qualitative researchers remain responsive to surprises in the field and adjust their inquiry accordingly
27
Mixed MethodsQual ResearchLooking Ahead
Theory

Self-Awareness

  • →"Who [a researcher is] — their gender, race, height and weight, level of extraversion, grace or awkwardness, hairstyle, clothing, vocabulary and intonation, class background, religion, fundamental beliefs, sympathy or disdain for others, and ignorance, and every other aspect of their identity — affects how easily they reach others, what others do and say in front of them, and how they interpret what they have heard or observed." (p. 119)
  • →Sound qualitative research requires the researcher to acknowledge and reflect on these dynamics
