Primary vs. secondary data — What

Primary

Original data collected directly for the present project.

Examples: interviews, surveys, focus groups, observations you conduct to answer a research question

Secondary

Data produced by someone else or collected for a separate purpose.

Examples: internet sources, NGO & government agencies, public libraries, educational institutions, commercial/business information

16
SimulationDebriefNext Steps

Primary vs. secondary data — Why (advantages)

Primary

  • •Data collected is first-hand
  • •Data collection can be controlled
  • •Method customized to the research question

Secondary

  • •Information is often readily available
  • •Less expensive and time-consuming
  • •Can aid in hypothesis testing and generalizability
17
SimulationDebriefNext Steps

Primary vs. secondary data — Why not (disadvantages)

Primary

  • •Can be expensive — time & resources
  • •One method is often not enough; mixed methods require more time, personnel

Secondary

  • •Credibility sometimes unknown or unverifiable
  • •May be dated
  • •Dependent on quality of prior research

Ethics of secondary data

Consent

Obtain permission or informed consent for any data that is not in the public domain.

De-identification

Can be much harder than it seems — very few details are needed to uniquely identify a person.

Attribution

Full and accurate acknowledgment of the source.

Emerging debate: Do tweets — or YouTube comments, or Reddit posts — require consent?

Case: El Salvador — Hoover Green

Hoover Green has conducted extensive desk research using over 60,000 incident-level records of political violence — both lethal and non-lethal — against civilians during the civil war in El Salvador (1980–1992).

The data — coded from then-confidential reports to NGOs, casualty lists from local groups, and Truth Commission data — include full names, specific places, and other identifying information for tens of thousands of Salvadorans, both victims and perpetrators, many of whom are still living.

Transparency vs. protection

Can you be appropriately transparent while still protecting subjects — none of whom consented to this particular use?

Aggregation & false precision

What level of aggregation avoids falsely precise conclusions from approximate numbers?
