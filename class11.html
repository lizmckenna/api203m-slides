What Is Qualitative Coding?

→Coding is the analytical workhorse for qualitative research

→Applying labels (or “codes”) to your data in order to find patterns across participants, evidence of interesting processes at play, and broader takeaways from the research

→Codes are the middle step that take us from raw data to a set of findings

Counting is not understanding

“To assess the plot of the three little pigs, no child would count the number of times that the wolf blew down a house … because the meaning of the three little pigs is in the story that is there to convey the meaning.”
Richard Biernacki, Reinventing Evidence in Social Inquiry

Natural-science coding uncovers patterns hidden from perception.
Cultural analysis coding can cover over meaning that was already there.

Competing Risk Framings AI as Cognitive Prosthetic Peer Influence on AI Adoption Fear of Capability Gap Gendered Technology Access
  • LLM-generated themes may look inductive, but they are not truly emerging from the data. Instead, they reflect patterns the model has already learned from vast prior texts about topics like AI, anxiety, gender, and work. Without a defined codebook, the model defaults to the most statistically common frames — not necessarily what is specific or novel in the data.
  • This means it can miss moments where the data resists easy categorization. Those moments of friction are where real insight and discovery happen.
  • For example, labeling a response as “Gendered Technology Access” may be technically accurate, but it overlooks the deeper context — such as how these dynamics play out within personal relationships and everyday life.

Same data. Different theory. Different discovery.

Gender lens

Wajcman, feminist technology studies

C names a gender gap: the only student to do so. Who has unstructured time to build capability? Whose domestic arrangements free them to experiment? The gap is not just skill. It is structural access to the conditions that produce skill.

Class and credential lens

Bourdieu

Students have invested enormously in what a Harvard MPP means as cultural capital. AI is destabilizing the conversion rate in real time. The field is changing the rules while they play. Learning loss is the surface. Credential devaluation is underneath.

Status loss lens

Relative deprivation

“When you said you don’t use it, my brain went, oh she must be so smart.” “I’m not smart enough to use it.” Status is relational, running in both directions. Non-use is read as sufficiency. Use is read as inadequacy. Not learning loss: status loss.

Deductive coding finds what you named in advance. Inductive coding finds patterns on the surface. Theory tells you what those patterns mean and why they matter.

GABRIEL is a measurement instrument

It takes a construct you have already defined: “how populist is this speech?” and measures how much of it is present consistently across thousands of observations.

It is a thermometer. Accurate. Consistent. Scalable.

It does not tell you what fever means.

Do not confuse the thermometer for a theory of fever.

Three CodingsMeasurement vs InterpretationSynthesis

What measurement at scale reveals

Congressional speeches 1880 to 2022: Democrats and Republicans were indistinguishable on progressive social attributes until 2008, then suddenly completely bifurcated. No human could have seen that across that volume.

37,000 technologies: adoption lags have fallen tenfold from 50 years in the 19th century to 5 years today. Nobody in the data knew that pattern. It was hidden until someone measured it at scale.

Three CodingsMeasurement vs InterpretationSynthesis

When data doesn’t know what it means, the instrument is the right tool

In STEM, the phenomena don’t have accounts of themselves. A protein doesn’t know it’s folding. A fault line doesn’t have a theory of earthquakes. The pattern was hidden and the instrument reveals it.

In cultural analysis, the meaning was already social before you arrived. A was already doing something when she said “I got to Harvard, I did it.” That meaning was live in the room.

When the data doesn’t know what it means: coverage and consistency are the virtues. When the data already knows what it means: your job is fidelity.

An instrument measures the contraction. It cannot tell them apart.

twitch

Two boys contract their right eyelids. Identical muscle movement. One is an involuntary twitch. One is a wink: conspiratorial, requiring shared context, an audience who can read it. Only a human reader who knows what winking means in this particular room can tell them apart.

Clifford Geertz, The Interpretation of Cultures

A’s guilt is not just a feeling. It is how she knows she still values something. Guilt is a moral boundary marker. It does work.

GABRIEL is powerful. Know what it costs.

GABRIEL is the right tool when

The data doesn’t know what it means. The pattern is structural and aggregate. You need to read 37,000 technologies or 140 years of congressional speech. No human can do that.

Human in vivo is the right tool when

The meaning was already social before you turned on the recorder. The sequence matters. The voice matters.

When you apply computational coding to cultural data you don’t just measure it: you transform it. You replace the social act with a variable. The wink becomes a twitch. The pride becomes anxiety.integrity resolved. You haven’t coded the data. You’ve substituted something legible for something true.

How I built this lesson with LLMs: the honest accounting

  • 01

    Whisper transcription of 11 focus groups, 3,674 utterances.

    Friction removed: hours of manual transcription. Cost: no intimacy with data. Acceptable for a teaching dataset. Not for book research.

  • 02

    LLM deductive + inductive coding of 135 quotes.

    Friction removed: applying codebook in seconds. Cost: efficiency creates illusion of thoroughness. Codes land but surprises don’t surface.

  • 03

    Building this HTML slide deck without learning CSS or JSON.

    Friction removed: significant. Cost: low. The friction was not doing useful work. Compare: my son used AI to skip the thinking. I used AI to skip the CSS. That is the distinction.

  • 04

    The car conversation: using live AI dialogue to organize the lesson while driving.

    Friction removed: commute became analytical work. Cost: driving alone meditatively might have produced something different. Possibly more surprising. The insight that arrives when nobody is asking you questions is a different cognitive mode.

Are you using AI well? Five questions

  • 01

    Are you building intuition about the underlying phenomenon, or just getting outputs?

  • 02

    Are you generating better questions, or fewer questions?

  • 03

    Could you defend every choice if someone pushed back?

  • 04

    Are you closer to the material or further from it?

  • 05

    Is this resistance building the capacity you came here to develop? If yes: keep it. If the friction is administrative, logistical, a tax on your attention that has nothing to do with the skill itself: remove it without guilt.

Your friction algorithm determines the researcher you become

The international student with the chatchapity accent decided her English voice was load-bearing friction.

The Air Force student decided the thinking process itself was why he was sent to HKS.

They are not wrong or right. They have made different bets about what kind of person they are becoming.

The question is not: should I use AI? The question is: what are you here to learn to do? Work backwards from that. Your friction algorithm follows.
