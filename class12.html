Takeaway 01

Qualitative research is a craft

  • →Practice, not just reading — you learned observation, interviewing, focus groups, and coding by doing them, not just reading about them
  • →Reflexivity matters — your positionality shapes what you see, what you ask, and what you conclude

Guest LectureTakeawaysFinal Assignment

Takeaway 02

Small & Calarco: what good looks like

  • →Small’s Someone Knows — showed us how interviews and ethnography can reveal mechanisms that surveys miss entirely
  • →Calarco’s Holding It Together — demonstrated how qualitative methods capture lived experience behind structural inequality
10
Guest LectureTakeawaysFinal Assignment

Takeaway 03

Generalizability & causality

  • →Different standards, not lower ones — qualitative research offers depth, mechanism, and context — not statistical generalizability, and that’s by design
  • →Causal claims require causal designs — qual work excels at theory-building and understanding how and why, which complements causal inference
11
Guest LectureTakeawaysFinal Assignment

Takeaway 04

Let the question drive the method

  • →Start with what you want to know — don’t pick a method first and then look for questions it can answer
  • →Mixed methods aren’t always better — they’re better when the research question demands multiple forms of evidence

Takeaway 05

There’s room for creativity

James’ point: The best qualitative work doesn’t just follow a template — it finds inventive ways to answer the question. The methods you choose should fit the world you’re studying, not the other way around.

Takeaway 06

Acknowledging limitations honestly

  • →Every method has trade-offs — the mark of a sophisticated researcher is naming them upfront, not burying them in a footnote
  • →Limitations are not apologies — they are a transparent account of what your design can and cannot tell us

Final Assignment

Mock PAE Proposal

  • →What is it? — A research proposal that identifies a policy-relevant question and designs a qualitative or mixed-methods study to answer it
  • →Why a proposal? — Writing a proposal forces you to connect question → method → data → analysis — the full pipeline we’ve practiced all semester
18
Guest LectureTakeawaysFinal Assignment

Rubric Highlights

Research Question

Clear, focused, policy-relevant, and answerable with qualitative or mixed methods

Methods Design

Appropriate method(s) for the question, with justified sampling, recruitment, and data collection plan

Analysis Plan

Coding strategy, analytical framework, and how you’ll move from data to findings

Ethics

IRB considerations, informed consent, positionality, and potential harms

Limitations

Honest assessment of trade-offs and what the design cannot tell us

Tips & Examples

  • →Draw on what we read — use Small, Calarco, Pager, or any of the course readings as models for how to frame a question and justify a method
  • →Be specific — “I will interview 15 community health workers in Nairobi” is stronger than “I will conduct interviews”
  • →Past student examples are on Canvas — look at what earned strong marks and why
